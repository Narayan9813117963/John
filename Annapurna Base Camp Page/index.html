    Trip Overview

    Hightlight of 10 Days Annapurna Base Camp Trek

    • Trek through lush rhododendron forest
    • Enjoy spectacular view of mountain masiff Annapurna (8091m) and its surounding peaks Machhapuchhre, Mardi Himal, Annapurna, Gangapurna, Singhchuli, Hiunchuli
    • Experience the unique culture, tradition and lifestyle of rural Nepalese
    • Enjoy mesmarizing sunrise from Poonhill

    The 10-day Annapurna Base Camp Trek is one of the most popular trekking packages in the Annapurna region among international and domestic trekkers. The 122-kilometer trail leads to the Annapurna base camp, which is 4131 meters above sea level. Our 10-day Annapurna Base Camp Trek is challenging but rewarding, and with proper planning and preparation, it can be a life-changing experience. This trek covers the major highlights of the Annapurna region: Poon Hill, Ghorepani Poon Hill Trek, and Annapurna Base Camp. The 10-day ABC Trek will be one of your most memorable treks, with breathtaking views of the high Himalayas and vast natural beauty.

    The Annapurna Base Camp Trek starts at Nayapul as you drive from Pokhara to Nayapul. Annapurna Base Camp Trek trail passes through beautiful villages, rivers, valleys,farmlands, Pastures, waterfalls, and lush sub-tropical forest to alpine meadow. During the 10 days of Annapurna Base Camp Trekking, you will have the chance to explore the unique traditions, cultures and lifestyle of the high Himalayan peoples and interact with them. The route, which is blanketed in blooming rhododendron, and the scenery of massive snow-capped peaks make you feel as if you are traveling to another world. The mesmerizing views of sunrise and sunset from Poonhill are one of the most famous parts of the trek. Many trekkers from around the globe dream of experiencing the sunset and sunrise from Poonhill over the giant White Mountains. This trek is also well-known for its panoramic view of massive snow-capped mountains from the Annapurna Base Camp.

    This Annapurna Base Camp Trek is an ideal package for those who want to experience the beautiful Himalayas and reach Annapurna Base Camp within a short time and within budget. We have carefully designed this trek so that you can experience the major highlights of the Annapurna Base Camp Trek while still arriving at ABC in 10 days. The Annapurna Base Camp Trek is completed after ten days of trekking with a true taste of Nepalese lifestyle and no difficulties with altitude sickness or walking. As the trek has a short duration and is an easy trek, anyone with stable health and at least 7 hours of walking capacity per day can join the trek and accomplish it without any difficulties.

    The 10-day Annapurna Base Camp Trek allows you to experience the culture and lifestyle of the Nepalese people while also seeing incredible views of giant mountains and sunrise and sunset views over them. The trek is suitable for both beginners and experienced trekkers, and with a good quality itinerary, packing list, and training plan, you can ensure a safe and comfortable trekking experience. With the right attitude and respect for the local culture and traditions, the Annapurna Base Camp Trek can be a once-in-a-lifetime adventure.

    Remeber us at Life Dream Adventure for other incredible trekking destinations like Everest Base Camp Trek, Annapurna Circuit Trek, Mardi Himal Trek, Langtang Valley Trek, Everest Three High Passes Trek and others.

    Day to Day Itinerary

    Day 1 : Drive from Pokhara to Nayapul and Trek to Tikhedhunga

    Food

    Breakfast, Lunch & Dinner

    Accomodation

    Lodge

    Elevation

    1560

    Morning accompanied with the breakfast and takes a drive for about 1 and half hour through large village Lumley to Nayapul. The trekking journey starts from here. The trail leads up passing through the beautiful rice, maize and millet farms. We will pass small beautiful waterfalls following main trail of Birethanti. Past waterfalls with plunge pools that invite you to swim. Then we reach Sudami village where we can have lunch. After lunch climb gradually side of valley to Hile and after half an hour working from Hile, you will reach at Tikhedhunga. Enjoy the local people, culture and greenery.

    Overnight in Tikhedhunga

    Day 2 : Trek from Tikhedhunga to Ghorepani

    Food

    Breakfast, Lunch & Dinner

    Accomodation

    Lodge

    Elevation

    1560

    After the breakfast, trek to Ghorepani (2880m) for approx. 7 hr. Today the trail crosses Tikhedunga River on suspension bridge, then drops and crosses the Burundi Khola. Climbing steeply on a stone staircase, continue to the large Magar village Ulleri (2080m). We will have a little view of mountain. We will pass through pastures and cultivated fields. We will encounter cool oak and Rhododendron forest through the journey. We have a little rest with lunch at Nangathanti. From here working for about one hour we will reach Ghorepani village. Arrive at the top of the Ghorepani pass to set your camp.

    Overnight in Ghorepani

    Day 3 : Trek from Ghorepani to Tadapani

    Food

    Breakfast, Lunch & Dinner

    Accomodation

    Lodge

    Elevation

    1560

    Following day make the early-morning departure to the Poon Hill (3210m) which is one of the best Himalaya view point in Nepal. You will be amazed to have the spectacular panoramic views of Himalayas of the region. It provides best majestic scenery and sunrise over the high mountains. Have the breathtaking views of Dhaulagiri I (8167m), Tukuche (6920m), Nilgiri (6940m), Annapurna South, Annapurna I (8091m), Mt. Fishtail (Machhapuchhre, 6997m), Lamjung Himal (6986m) and many more.

    Now return to the camp at Ghorepani and have your breakfast. Trek to Tadapani starts for rest of the day for 6 hours. You will have amazing views of Dhaulagiri and Annapurna on the way. Descend steeply through dense moss-covered forest rich with bird species.

    Overnight in Tadapani

    Breakfast, Lunch & Dinner

From here there are two trails one takes us to Ghandruk while other to Chhomrong, we will follow the trail to Chhomrong. Crossing the sever rivers and passing through the lust forests and terraced fields we will reach Taulung. A steep descent from Taulung will take us to one of the most beautiful village Chhomrong. After a shrt descent from Chhomrong we will cross a suspension bridge and ascend to Sinuwa.

We will have the closest view of Machhapuchhre, Mardi Himal, Annapurna, Gangapurna, Singhchuli, Hiunchuli etc from Annapurna Base Camp.

    FAQs

    The Annapurna Base Camp trek is a popular trekking route in Nepal that takes you through diverse landscapes, from lush forests to high-altitude terrains, to reach the base camp of Annapurna, one of the world's highest mountains.

    The best time to go for the Annapurna Base Camp trek is during the spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November) seasons. The weather during these months is generally clear, dry, and mild, making it easier to trek.

    The Annapurna Base Camp trek usually takes around 7 to 12 days, depending on the trekker's pace and the route taken.

    The Annapurna Base Camp trek is considered a moderate to difficult trek. The altitude, terrain, and duration of the trek can make it challenging, but with proper training and preparation, it is achievable for most people.

    Some essential items to pack for the Annapurna Base Camp trek include comfortable hiking shoes, warm clothes, rain gear, a first aid kit, a trekking pole, a sleeping bag, and a headlamp.

    It is not mandatory to hire a guide for the Annapurna Base Camp trek, but it is highly recommended, especially for inexperienced trekkers. A guide can help you navigate the trails, provide valuable information about the culture and history of the region, and ensure your safety.

    The cost of the Annapurna Base Camp trek varies depending on several factors, such as the duration of the trek, the route taken, the season, and the level of accommodation. On average, the cost can range from $500 to $1500 per person.

    Accommodation during the Annapurna Base Camp trek ranges from basic teahouses to more luxurious lodges. Most teahouses offer shared rooms and bathrooms, while some lodges offer private rooms and hot showers.

    To prepare for the Annapurna Base Camp trek, you should focus on building your endurance, strength, and flexibility. This can include activities such as cardio exercises, hiking, yoga, and weight training.

    Yes, altitude sickness can be a concern during the Annapurna Base Camp trek, especially as you climb to higher elevations. It is essential to acclimatize properly and listen to your body's signals to avoid altitude sickness.

    Yes, it is generally safe to trek to Annapurna Base Camp, provided you follow the proper safety precautions, such as trekking with a guide, carrying a first aid kit, and staying hydrated.

    While previous trekking experience can be helpful, it is not mandatory to do the Annapurna Base Camp trek. With proper training, preparation, and guidance, most people can complete the trek.

    Additional Information

    The Annapurna Base Camp Trek starts from Nayapul and follows a well-defined trail through the villages of Tikhedhunga, Ghorepani, and Tadapani. From Tadapani, the trail leads to Chomrong and then to the Modi Khola river, which is followed until reaching the Annapurna Sanctuary.

    The trail to the Annapurna Base Camp goes through Machhapuchhre Base Camp and offers stunning views of the Annapurna range, including Annapurna South, Annapurna I, Hiunchuli, Gangapurna, and Machhapuchhre. After spending a night at the base camp, the trek retraces the same trail back to Nayapul.

    The cost of the Annapurna Base Camp Trek can vary depending on various factors such as the season, the type of accommodation, and the mode of transportation. On average, the cost of the trek can range from $700 to $1500.

    The cost includes the permit fees, guide and porter fees, transportation, food, and accommodation. The cost can be reduced by trekking independently and staying in budget-friendly teahouses instead of expensive lodges.

    The Annapurna Base Camp Trek itinerary can vary depending on the trekker's preferences and time availability. The standard itinerary for the trek is as follows:

    Day 1: Drive from Pokhara to Nayapul and trek to Tikhedhunga Day 2: Trek to Ghorepani Day 3: Early morning hike to Poon Hill for sunrise and panoramic views. Trek to Tadapani Day 4: Trek to Chomrong Day 5: Trek to Himalaya Day 6: Trek to Annapurna Base Camp Day 7: Trek back to Bamboo Day 8: Trek to Jhinu Danda Day 9: Trek to Nayapul and drive back to Pokhara

    The Annapurna Base Camp Trek is a moderate to difficult trek, with some steep ascents and descents. The trek involves hiking through challenging terrain, including steep stone steps, narrow trails, and suspension bridges.

    The altitude gain is gradual, with the highest point being the Annapurna Base Camp at an elevation of 4130 meters. However, it is essential to acclimatize properly and take necessary precautions to avoid altitude sickness.
